Program Details

Education Access & Aid

A deep dive into how we provide proper learning facilities in remote villages, overcoming challenges like damaged buildings and lack of internet access.

The Challenge

In many remote areas of Indonesia, children face significant barriers to education. School buildings are often damaged and unsafe, lacking basic sanitation, electricity, and internet access. Furthermore, a shortage of qualified teachers and a lack of learning materials like textbooks and uniforms mean that children are not getting the quality education they deserve.

Our Solution

Our "Education Access & Aid" program tackles these issues head-on. We work with our local partner, Lentera Aksara Negeri Foundation, to identify communities most in need.

  • School Renovation: We fund the repair of damaged classrooms, build new sanitation facilities, and work to provide solar power for electricity.
  • Learning Materials: We distribute packages of schoolbooks, notebooks, pens, and school uniforms, removing the financial barrier for poor families.
  • Teacher Support: We provide resources and training for local volunteer teachers to help them deliver engaging lessons.

"Seeing a child receive their first new backpack and set of books is a moment of pure joy. It's not just supplies; it's a message that their future matters."

Volunteer, West Sumatra

Your Impact

Your donation directly contributes to this change. A simple contribution can have a massive impact:

  • €25: Provides a complete school kit (uniform, shoes, bag, books) for one child.
  • €100: Funds one month of training and allowance for a local teacher.
  • €500: Can purchase new desks and chairs for an entire classroom.

Program Facts
  • Location: Remote villages (e.g., Alor, West Sumatra)
  • Focus: Children (Ages 6-15)
  • Key Areas: Infrastructure, Learning Materials, Teacher Support
Our Partner

This program is implemented on the ground in partnership with the Lentera Aksara Negeri Foundation, ensuring all aid is culturally respectful and effective.
